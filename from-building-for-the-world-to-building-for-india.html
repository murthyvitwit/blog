From building for the world to building for India

July 24, 2026

For the last decade, I built from India for the world.

Today, I’m building from India for India.

For the last 11 years at Vitwit, we built battle-tested blockchain infrastructure used by projects around the world.

Through Kathanika and our first product, Kriya AI, I’m building AI products for India.

Why?

Because I believe India has its own problems and needs its own AI products.

Most AI companies today are building for a global audience. People who are comfortable with English and familiar with global culture.

That market is massive.

But India is different.

Millions of people and businesses want to create and express themselves through AI in their native languages, for their festivals, families, communities and everyday lives.

They don’t need more AI features.

They need an AI platform that removes friction from what they already do.

I believe this is one of the biggest opportunities in AI over the next decade.

Kriya is our first step toward that vision.

It’s only been 100 days.

We’ve crossed 100,000 downloads, reached $1,000 MRR, and over $3,000 in monthly tracked revenue with a very small team.

This is just the beginning.

Right now, I’m focused on solving the next 10x problem, going from $100 in daily revenue to $1,000 in daily revenue.

This is the first time I’m sharing this journey publicly.

If you’re building for India, I’d love to connect and learn from your journey.

If you’re an investor who believes the next generation of AI companies will be built by deeply understanding Indian users, I’d be happy to connect too. We’re not fundraising today, but I’m always looking for thoughtful conversations and long-term relationships.

The next generation of AI companies won’t just be built in India.

They’ll be built for India.
